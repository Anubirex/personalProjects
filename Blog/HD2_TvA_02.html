Decoding the Challenge: Termanids vs. Automatons in Helldivers 2

Like countless others who have navigated the perilous terrains of Helldivers 2, I've wondered which adversary poses the greater challenge: the Termanids or the Automatons? With over 200 hours of gameplay under my belt, I've arrived at a conclusion. While I understand that this perspective might not sit well with enthusiasts of the Termanids, maintaining objectivity is crucial. The conclusion I've reached, through extensive gameplay, is that Automatons present a significantly higher challenge than their bug counterparts, and frankly, the difference is quite stark. Here's the rationale behind my viewpoint:

Loadout Versatility: Tackling the Tactical Divide Between Bugs and Automatons

The versatility in loadouts that proves effective against bugs illustrates this point. I've successfully employed a variety of weapons and stratagems, from rail guns and flamethrowers to arc throwers (exceptionally effective against bugs) and Expendable Anti-Tank stratagems. These combinations remain efficient across all levels of difficulty, showcasing the adaptability players have when facing bug enemies, who are limited to melee attacks or short-range spits. Conversely, Automatons introduce a heightened level of difficulty with their predominantly ranged attacks, employing lasers, rockets, and mortars, among others. This fundamental difference in combat approach elevates every Automaton unit to a more formidable threat than any bug, as Automatons only need a line of sight to attack. In contrast, bugs necessitate proximity for their attacks, offering players the strategy of constant movement to evade close encounters. Even amidst the chaos of multiple charging bugs, as long as players possess a suitable support weapon and maintain mobility, dodging becomes a feasible survival tactic, underscoring the comparative ease in managing bug confrontations.

Strategic Shifts: Navigating the Complexity of Combat Against Automatons

Engaging the Termanids offers a uniquely enjoyable experience where strategy revolves around maintaining distance and utilizing almost any weapons or stratagems to clear the field. This dynamic shifts dramatically when facing the Automatons, as the battle tactics and equipment selection become more critical and constrained. Against Automatons, not every arsenal combination proves effective. Each Automaton adversary is armored, requiring precise shots to their weak points—typically the head—to be taken down effectively. The challenge escalates with the frequent spawning of Hulks and tanks in higher difficulty levels, where even a single one of these enemies can significantly complicate the battlefield. The situation intensifies when faced with a combination of four Hulks, two tanks, eight Devastators of various types, and numerous standard combat units, all ready to fire upon the slightest visibility of a player. Eliminating these foes demands specific tactics, such as targeting the Hulks' heads with multiple rocket shots and aiming for the back of tanks' cannons. This onslaught forces a direct confrontation as they advance towards you, markedly increasing the complexity of the encounter.

Redefining Challenge: The Tactical Rigor of Automaton Combat in Helldivers 2

In contrast to battles against the Termanids, confrontations with the Automatons severely limit player movement due to the enemies' ranged capabilities. Additionally, the necessity to constantly address the threat of armored units narrows down the viable choices of stratagems and weapons. This makes engagements with the Automatons not just a different kind of challenge, but a substantially more demanding one, where strategic depth and equipment selection are crucial to survival. Enemies clad in heavy armor present a unique challenge, requiring players to venture into vulnerability to target their weak spots. Engaging any enemy, in essence, necessitates revealing oneself, at which point every automaton in the vicinity commences an onslaught. In contrast, battling bugs offers a degree of freedom, allowing players to navigate and strategize kills against armored adversaries without much hindrance, effortlessly evading lesser foes in the process. Against bugs, almost any weapon proves effective, facilitating the rapid clearance of large swarms with relative ease. The automatons' vulnerability lies in their heads, a diminutive, spherical target that shifts with their movements, making it a difficult mark even when they are at a standstill. However, automatons seldom remain idle; they advance aggressively, firing as they come, compelling players to execute precisely aimed shots to their moving target to neutralize the threat. The disparity in challenge between fighting bugs and automatons is unmistakable. Frankly, I am of the view that those who consider bugs to pose a comparable challenge likely have not engaged with high-level automatons. While I don't profess to be the most adept Helldiver, my experiences speak volumes. I present my perspective not as an absolute verdict but as a basis for comparison, inviting you to assess the difficulty based on your own encounters.

Personal Combat Analysis: The Stark Contrast Between Termanids and Automatons

Having engaged in battles against both adversaries for roughly 100 hours apiece, my experiences draw a clear contrast between the two. In confrontations with bugs, I comfortably ramp up the difficulty to 9, consistently concluding missions with upwards of 400 kills and rarely succumbing more than once. Conversely, when facing off against automatons, a reduction in difficulty to 7 becomes necessary, and even then, I find myself grappling to secure 150-200 kills while averaging four to five fatalities. The memory of failing a mission against bugs due to a depletion of lives eludes me, yet just in recent sessions against automatons, we experienced mission failures on three separate occasions because we ran out of lives. The discrepancy of playing against bugs easily at level 9, while not being able to go beyond level 7 against automatons makes the answer of which is more difficult quite obvious. There is no need to delve into detailed analyses of enemy types or the strategic use of stratagems, the conclusion is starkly evident: automatons pose a substantially greater challenge.

Beyond Debate: The Clear Challenge of Automatons Over Termanids

In essence, framing this discussion as a debate seems almost redundant, as the comparison hardly warrants one. The question boils down to whether one finds it more challenging to battle against squishy, melee-only bugs or to take on armored robots equipped with scanners, capable of targeting you from a distance. And that's without even considering the additional challenges posed by the automatons, such as cannon turrets scattered across the map, mortar outposts raining down bombs, anti-air outposts that thwart aerial support, or the stratagem-jamming outposts that inhibit your tactical options. The automatons boast an array of technology specifically designed to counter Helldivers, turning each encounter into a rigorous test of strategy and prowess. In contrast, the bugs' strategy is merely to close in for a hit, and that's the extent of it. So, to conclusively address the inquiry of which foe presents a greater challenge, the Termanids or the Automatons, I assert without a doubt that it's the Automatons. Their level of difficulty is not only more pronounced, but the disparity is so vast that I find it baffling that there's any debate on the matter.
